Well Log Curves – Engineering Explanation

DEPT – Depth

Represents measured depth of the well (ft or m). All log measurements are plotted against depth.

Logged Interval = Max Depth − Min Depth

DT – Sonic Log (ΔT)

Measures acoustic travel time through formation (μs/ft). Used to estimate porosity.

Φ = (ΔTlog − ΔTmatrix) / (ΔTfluid − ΔTmatrix)

Where:

  • Φ = Porosity
  • ΔTlog = Measured sonic travel time
  • ΔTmatrix = Travel time in rock matrix
  • ΔTfluid = Travel time in fluid

RESD – Deep Resistivity

Measures electrical resistivity of formation (ohm·m). Used to detect hydrocarbons.

Swⁿ = (a × Rw) / (Φᵐ × Rt)

Archie’s Equation for water saturation calculation.

SP – Spontaneous Potential

Measures natural electrical potential between borehole and formation (mV).

SSP = −K log10(Rmf / Rw)

Used for lithology identification and estimating formation water resistivity.

GR – Gamma Ray

Measures natural radioactivity (API units). Used to differentiate shale from sand.

IGR = (GRlog − GRmin) / (GRmax − GRmin)

Gamma Ray Index (IGR) used to estimate shale volume (Vsh).
